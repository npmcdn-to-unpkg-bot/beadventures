Day 1:
Bako National Park
Nature logo
{# what dis? #} 45 mins drive
20 mins boat ride

Depart the hotel at 8am. Breakfast on infamous Sarawak laksa, popiah or rot I at a popular local cafe ( Choon Hui or Choon Chou )

Your journey to Bako National Park is made all the more pleasurable with a 20min boat ride through a stilted water village bustling with local fisherman.

As the inlet opens to the calm waters of the South China Sea we pass traditional fish traps, mangroves and islands.

Look out for the bottle nose dolphin, crocodiles, water monitors and coastal sea eagles.

Bako National Park provides the perfect introduction to your wildlife encounter with regular sightings of Proboscis monkey, long tailed macaque, silverleaf monkeys and wild boar.

Explore a trail or two and enjoy a picnic lunch before returning to town.

Behind the scenes at Matang rehabilitation sanctuary.

The dedicated staff and volunteers at Matang have saved and rehabitated thousands of endangered Bornean animals since their inception in....

Day 3:
Depart 8am for a full day of Borneo at its best. Kick start with the much anticipated visit to Semengoh Wildlife Sanctuary to witness orangutan in relatively nature setting. Back that up with a kayak journey along the...stopping at scenic locations for swimming, sightseeing and a traditional longhouse lunch.

On our last night in Kuching we learn we take a cooking class and share our meal with some Kuching locals doing great work for the people, animal and protected areas of Sarawak.

Day 4 - Day 8:
Kuching - Mulu World Heritage Area
Over the next 4 days in Mulu our time is divided between sight seeing, working on volunteer projects at Mulu National Park and S.K. Batu Bungan,( the local boarding school and home to around 120 Orang Ulu students from the remote tributaries of the Baram River.)

Volunteer projects vary depending on weather, community needs and priorities and can be catered to the groups passion and experience. See partnerships and volunteer projects or contact us for further details on current projects.

Tours in and around Mulu World Heritage Area are coordinated with consideration to volunteer projects but include the following iconic experience; Clearwater Cave, Deer Cave, the Bat Exudus, night walk tour and visit to local Longhouse and art market. Please contact us if you would like to take part in an adventure caving experience/ extended day trip such as Pinnacles Climb, Clearwater Revival Adventure Caving or Garden of Eden Valley Walk
